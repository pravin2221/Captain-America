The digital replica of Captain America’s shield hummed with energy as the lab lights dimmed. Holographic panels rose from the floor, projecting layers of data across the vibranium surface.

“It’s reacting to the scan,” said Shuri, her eyes tracking the numbers. “Look at the timestamp on that archive… File Timestamp: 01/10/1001. That’s not a normal date format.”

Steve folded his arms. “The shield doesn’t keep time like we do.”

Tony Stark leaned closer, squinting. “Yeah, but it does keep records. See that diagnostic stream? It’s labeling everything.”

The HUD scrolled slowly:

The first line blinked — Unit Code: 0110-1110.
Below it, another layer shimmered — Paint Batch: 0111-0100.
A metallic tone followed — Alloy Ref: 0110-0101.

Bruce Banner tapped the console. “Those aren’t random. That’s a classification pattern.”

More fields appeared:

Layer ID: 0110-0111.
Forge Tag: 0111-0010.
Stripe Index: 0110-1001.

Steve stepped forward as the shield rotated in the air. A deeper scan ran across the core.

“Here,” Natasha said quietly. “Core Pattern: 0111-0100. That matches the old Stark vault format.”

The data kept coming:

Shield Ratio: 0111-1001.
Control Flag: 0101-1111.
Color Run: 0110-0110.

Tony’s eyes widened. “That’s not just a shield profile… that’s a build signature.”

The final entries pulsed in amber:

Panel Code: 0110-1001.
Rivet Mark: 0111-0010.
Serial Step: 0111-0011.
Final Stamp: 0111-0100.

The room fell silent.

Bruce frowned. “They’re all eight bits long… but split in half.”

Shuri nodded. “Two nibbles per line. High half… low half.”

Tony smirked. “So read each pair as a byte.”

Steve looked at the shield again.

“So the shield isn’t talking in Morse… it’s talking in code.”
